📊 Telecom Customer Churn Analysis
Project Overview

This project analyzes customer churn behavior in a telecom company and identifies the key factors responsible for customer attrition. The objective is to help the business improve customer retention, reduce revenue loss, and identify high-risk customer segments.

Business Problem

Customer churn is a major challenge for telecom companies because acquiring new customers is more expensive than retaining existing ones.

This project answers:

Why are customers leaving?
Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
What is the revenue impact of churn?
How can the company improve customer retention?

Key Findings
Customer Churn Rate
Overall Churn Rate: 26.54%
High-Risk Segments
New Customer Churn Rate: 47.44%
Electronic Check Churn Rate: 45.29%
Month-to-Month Contract Churn Rate: 42.71%
Fiber Optic Churn Rate: 41.89%
Customer Retention Insights
Customers with longer tenure are significantly less likely to churn.
Higher monthly charges increase churn risk.
Long-term customers generate higher lifetime value.
Revenue Insights
Revenue Lost Due to Churn: ₹28.63 Lakh
Two-Year Contract customers generated the highest customer value.

Result:

Business Recommendations
Convert Month-to-Month customers into long-term contracts.
Focus retention efforts during the first 12 months.
Promote AutoPay adoption.
Bundle Online Security services.
Improve Fiber Optic customer experience.
Target High-Risk Customers through retention campaigns.
Use predictive analytics for early churn detection.
Establish customer feedback programs to reduce dissatisfaction.
